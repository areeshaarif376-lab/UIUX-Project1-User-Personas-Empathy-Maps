# Project 1 – User Personas & Empathy Maps

## Objective

The objective of this project was to understand the challenges faced by busy professionals who struggle to maintain a fitness routine and to create User Personas and Empathy Maps before moving into the design phase.

---

# Research Approach

Instead of making assumptions, I conducted AI-assisted interview simulations and approached the conversations as a UX researcher.

The interviews focused on understanding:

- Daily routines
- Work-life balance
- Motivations
- Fitness habits
- Emotional barriers
- User goals and frustrations

The findings were then translated into user personas and empathy maps.

---

# Persona 1 – Ali Raza

## Profile

- Age: 28
- Occupation: Marketing Executive
- Location: Karachi, Pakistan

## Selected Interview Questions & Insights

### What do you struggle with most during your day?

> “By the time I get home from work, I’m completely drained.”

### What do you think about exercise?

> “I know it’s important, but it feels like another task after a long day.”

### What was your gym experience like?

> “I felt out of place because everyone seemed to know what they were doing.”

### What would make fitness easier to stick to?

> “Short workouts, simple instructions, and something that fits naturally into my routine.”

### What would your ideal fitness app look like?

> “I want something simple that tells me exactly what to do without making fitness feel overwhelming.”

## Key Findings

- Low energy after work
- Long commute
- Difficulty maintaining consistency
- Gym feels intimidating
- Prefers low-effort activities

### UX Insight

> “I don’t need intense workouts. I need something I can actually stick to.”

---

# Persona 2 – Sana Khan

## Profile

- Age: 34
- Occupation: Human Resources Manager
- Location: Karachi, Pakistan

## Selected Interview Questions & Insights

### What motivates you in your job?

> “Helping people and making things run smoothly.”

### What is the thing you want the most in life?

> “A healthy and balanced life where I can achieve my goals without sacrificing my own well-being.”

### What decision do you regret the most?

> “I kept telling myself I would take care of myself later.”

### Are your children cooperative?

> “They are supportive, but they still need my time and attention.”

### What would motivate you to use a fitness app?

> “Don’t make fitness feel like another responsibility.”

### Which exercises would you actually enjoy?

> “Walking, stretching, chair exercises, and activities I can do with my family.”

### What features would you love in a fitness app?

> “Family challenges, short activities, stress-relief features, and flexible routines.”

## Key Findings

- Family responsibilities take priority
- Limited personal time
- Struggles with work-life balance
- Prefers family-friendly wellness activities
- Needs flexibility rather than intensity

### UX Insight

> “I need fitness to fit into the time I already spend with my family.”

# What I Learned

This project taught me that effective UX design begins with understanding people before designing solutions.

Two users may face the same problem, but for completely different reasons:

- Ali struggled with energy and consistency.
- Sana struggled with balancing personal wellness and family responsibilities.

Creating these personas and empathy maps helped me understand the importance of empathy, research, and user-centered thinking in the design process.
